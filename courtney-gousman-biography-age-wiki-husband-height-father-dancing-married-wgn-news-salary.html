Courtney Gousman Biography-Wiki

Courtney Gousman is an American Emmy-nominated journalist who joined the WGN/ CLTV family in July of the year 2014. She was a former CLTV intern.

You can find her reporting the last breaking news on WGN Morning News. Courtney previously served as the sole Midday Anchor for CLTV News, delivering live coverage of breaking events happening in Chicagoland as well as around the world, throughout the day. Courtney also contributes special reports for WGN News, educating the community on little known-facts as well as shedding light on issues that are often overlooked.

Prior to Courtney’s time at WGN & CLTV, Courtney reported for WBBM-TV in Chicago, where Courtney won a 2014 Illinois Associated Press Award. She also spent time at St. Louis’ KSDK-TV as an Anchor or Reporter, bringing home a Missouri Broadcaster’s Award.

Among Courtney’s first on-air positions, Courtney spent time at WEHT-TV in Evansville, IN, and KRCG-TV in Jefferson City, MO.

She launched into broadcasting during her freshman year of college, while enrolled in the Scripps-Howard School of Journalism at Hampton University, working as a DJ for WHOV-FM in Hampton, VA.

She is proud of her south side roots— growing up in Chicago’s Beverly neighborhood. Courtney’s also honored to be an alum of Whitney Young High School on the city’s west side.

She is an active member of the National Association of Black Journalists (NABJ), Alpha Kappa Alpha Sorority, Inc, as well as has volunteered with Big Brothers Big Sisters of America.

Courtney’s self-proclaimed “foodie,” who loves to cook and explore new restaurants with her husband. She’s also very active on social media, as well as loves to connect with people on Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram.
