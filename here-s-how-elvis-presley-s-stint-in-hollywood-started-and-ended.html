Here's How Elvis Presley's Stint In Hollywood Started And Ended

 Posted on September 15, 2024  |  5 minutes  |  900 words  |  Larita Shotwell

Born in Mississippi, Elvis Presley first found his calling with music at a church in his neighborhood at a young age. The family moved to Tennessee, where he trained himself to play the guitar. While working with Sun Records, he worked part-time as a truck driver just when he released That’s All Right which became a massive hit. His dance moves and charisma on stage made Elvis Presley a popular entertainer amongst female audiences, and he landed a record deal with RCA valued at $40,000 in 1955.

After signing the deal, Presley released albums under the record label and created new music with Rock. The genre was later labeled Rock n’ Roll, with Elvis being called its King. He gained success with mainstream music as youths loved his screen presence which also helped him land movie roles to bring his skills to the theaters. While Presley had a steady acting career, he went from music to movies to music again. Let’s take a look at why Elvis Presley made a return to music after movies.

8 He Always Had A Passion For Cinema

Elvis Presley loved movies and the way they could influence people. From a young age, he found an interest in starring in a film. Before Tom Parker was signed on as Elvis’ manager, he contacted casting agencies with the hopes of starring the singer in a movie. The simple conversation with New York’s William Morris Agency morphed into a career for Elvis Presley in Hollywood. He actively worked in movies from 1956 to 1972.

7 He Wanted To Be A Leading Man

The legendary singer was hesitant about making his on-screen debut, but with multiple production houses willing to pay large amounts, Tom Parker negotiated lucrative deals worth millions. In 1956, he signed onto two production houses: 20th Century Fox and Paramount. Presley made his big screen debut as a supporting actor in Love Me Tender in 1956 under Fox. He made $100,000 for the role and $150,000 and $200,000 for later films.

6 His Exploration Beyond Music

Tom Parker helped Elvis Presley become a leading man and negotiated and renegotiated 16 contracts with different production companies to pay him for his worth. Due to his success in music, people, especially women, flocked the movie theaters to make each movie starring Elvis Presley a resounding success. Along with Paramount and 20th Century Fox, he also worked with MGM, Allied Artists, United Artists, NBC-Universal, and National General.

5 He Defined The Elvis Genre

With the success of Love Me Tender, production houses realized that teenage-oriented movies could also become popular. As the supporting role of a rock ‘n roll artist in his debut, Presley was cast in similar roles in the future. He plays a singer in King Creole, an ex-con pop star in Jailhouse Rock, a country boy in Wild in the Country, and a musical race driver in Viva Las Vegas. These films introduced the Elvis genre, which featured music interludes and comedy romances.

4 The War That Changed Everything

As the second World War raged, Elvis Presley was drafted into the US Army to serve his nation. Presley was at the peak of his career, and when he was offered to become a part of the Special Services to entertain the soldiers and live in a separate housing, Presley declined. He served as a regular soldier, earning the respect of people everywhere. Presley’s dance moves were regarded as vulgar and sexual, but the war changed people’s outlook on him.

3 Making 30 Movies In Eight Years

While Tom Parker dealt with 16 contracts that made him hundreds and millions, Elvis Presley put his head down and continued making movies. While not all of his films were well-received, they were successful commercially. Elvis’ father, Vernon, detailed that his son never watched any of his movies. One of the reasons was that Elvis didn’t like his projects, but they paid him handsomely.

2 He Enjoyed Making Music And Made A Comeback In 1968

As movies barely interested Elvis Presley, music was his second home. He found solace in creating music that helped people escape reality. His music peaked in the 1950s, but the singer returned in 1968 with a television special. He played his biggest hits while simultaneously paying tribute to gospel music. The made-for-TV special returned Presley to music which he would continue to do for the rest of his life.

1 He Felt Energized While Performing

With the new wave of music introduced in the 1960s, Elvis Presley found himself staggering a little with his music career. After his Comeback Special, the singer released a single Suspicious Minds, that peaked at No.1 on the charts. He earned a following after performing shows and concerts in America. Presley began his Las Vegas Residency in 1969, which continued for the next seven years until his death in 1976.

While some believe that Elvis Presley’s Hollywood career took him away from music, it also gave some of the biggest hits of his career. The musician switched careers, but his strive to create music remained active. Today, the singer inspires many aspiring artists who strive to bring innovation to genres.
