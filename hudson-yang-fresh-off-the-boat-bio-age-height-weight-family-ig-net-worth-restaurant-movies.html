Hudson Yang Fresh Off the Boat

Hudson began his performing career only 18 months prior to landing his starring role on Fresh Off The Boat. His first role came in the 2014 thriller, The Sisterhood of Night, in which Yang played the role of Henry Huang. He appeared on The View on February 3, 2015, ahead of the show’s debut and portrayed Henry in the mystery drama film. He currently stars in the television series Fresh Off the Boat as Randall Park Eddie Huang’s son.

The television series Fresh Off the Boat was picked up by ABC on May 10, 2014,  and premiered on February 4, 2015, with the first of the two preview episodes garnering 7.93 million viewers, becoming the second-highest-rated comedy premiere that season. Due to his acting prowess, Yang has been featured in a variety of shows, including Family Guy, Sofia the First, Liv and Maddie, The Lion Guard, and PBS’s Cyberchase.

The series premiered its first two episodes on ABC in February 2015 to positive critical reception, becoming the first network television sitcom in the U.S. to feature a family of Asian Americans as main characters in over 20 years. Prior to its second season, the series went through a significant retooling, which included Huang’s departure as narrator.

It has also received accolades as well as nominations for major awards, such as Critics’ Choice Television Award and NAACP Image Award nominations. After becoming the first series featuring an all Asian American main cast to broadcast over 100 episodes, it concluded on February 21, 2020, after six seasons.

Despite his tender years, Yang already has an impressive number of awards and nominations under his belt, including several NAACP Image Awards nominations for Outstanding Performance by a Youth, Teen Choice Award nominations for Choice Scene Stealer in 2016 and Outstanding TV Comedy Actor in 2017, and 2018. In 2016, he picked up the Award for Leading Teen Actor at the Young Artist Awards.
