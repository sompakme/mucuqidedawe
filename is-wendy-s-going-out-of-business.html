Is Wendys Going out of Business?

 Posted on September 23, 2024  |  15 minutes  |  3007 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

Rumors and misinformation have been circulating online regarding the potential closure or bankruptcy of Wendy’s, the popular fast-food restaurant chain. However, these claims are false and misleading.

Several online advertisements on social media platforms, like Facebook and Instagram, have hinted at Wendy’s closing down. However, these ads have been deemed inaccurate and misleading. They featured a photo of Wendy’s founder Dave Thomas and captions suggesting restaurant chains closing in general, without specifically mentioning that Wendy’s itself was going out of business. Furthermore, some of these ads referred to articles from 2020, making them outdated and irrelevant to the current situation.

It is important to clarify that Wendy’s is not going out of business. What actually happened is that a franchisee operator named NPC International, which operates some Wendy’s locations, filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy in July 2020. The sale of NPC International’s Wendy’s restaurants to Flynn Restaurant Group and existing franchisees has been approved, ensuring that these locations will remain open. In fact, Wendy’s has continued to open new restaurants, indicating a positive outlook for the company.

The origins of these false rumors can be traced back to misleading Facebook advertisements featuring photos of Wendy’s founder Dave Thomas. Bankruptcy Filing by Wendy’s Franchisee

Starboard Group, a restaurant operator based in Coral Springs, Florida, which operates several Wendy’s locations, has recently filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection. This franchisee operated approximately 61 Wendy’s restaurants in the Southeast region, including Florida, Alabama, and other states.

The bankruptcy filing does not impact Starboard Group’s other restaurant brands, such as Fuzzy’s Taco Shop, McAlister’s Deli, and Subway. The CEO of Starboard Group mentioned that the decision to file for bankruptcy was made in order to save jobs, strengthen the company, and ensure a more stable future.

The filing was attributed to a combination of factors, including post-COVID consumer habits, increasing operational costs, and higher interest rates. Despite these challenges, Starboard Group remains committed to the success of its Wendy’s locations and is working towards a successful restructuring plan.

The Impact of NPC International’s Bankruptcy

NPC International, a franchisee operator of Wendy’s and Pizza Hut locations, filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy in July 2020. This bankruptcy filing raised concerns about the future of NPC International’s Wendy’s locations. However, it was later announced that NPC International had sold its Wendy’s restaurants to Flynn Restaurant Group and existing franchisees. The sale was approved by a judge in 2021, ensuring that these locations would remain open under new ownership. Though the financial troubles of NPC International had an impact on some Wendy’s restaurants, the overall stability of the brand and the sale of these locations have mitigated any significant negative effects.

The Strategy Behind Misleading Ads on Social Media

The presence of misleading ads about Wendy’s closure on social media can be attributed to a strategy known as advertising arbitrage. Advertising arbitrage involves using initial clickbait ads to attract users to lengthy articles, with the hope of generating more revenue from the ads displayed in the articles. In the case of Wendy’s closure rumors, the ads featuring Wendy’s founder Dave Thomas and captions implying the closure of all Wendy’s restaurants were designed to entice users to click and read the articles. However, these articles did not provide accurate information about Wendy’s closure and were outdated. It is important to be cautious of clickbait ads on social media and to verify information from reliable sources before drawing conclusions.

The Clickbait Strategy

Clickbait is a strategy used by advertisers to attract users’ attention and encourage them to click on a specific article or advertisement. The ads mentioned earlier used clickbait techniques by featuring sensational claims about Wendy’s closure, which enticed users to click and read the articles. This strategy aims to generate higher revenue by driving more traffic to these articles, as advertisers can earn revenue from the ads displayed within them. However, it is essential to recognize that clickbait ads often contain misleading or false information, as seen in the case of Wendy’s closure rumors.
