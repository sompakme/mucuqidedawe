Jelly Roll, born Jason Deford, is a renowned American rapper, musician, and songwriter from Nashville, Tennessee.

He is popular as one of the pioneers of the white rap music genre in the Southern rap industry.

Jelly Roll’s rapping career is not only his passion but an excellent source of income, and he has amassed a considerable net worth through his successful musical journey. 

In this article, we will take a comprehensive look at Jelly Roll net worth, career, and how he made his fortune.

Early Life and Music Career

Jelly Roll was born on December 4th, 1984, in Nashville, Tennessee. He grew up in a family that primarily had a musical background, which influenced his musical interest at an early age. 

Jelly Roll was passionate about music and always dreamed of becoming one of the best rappers in the world. 

He started his journey in rap music by collaborating with Lil Wyte on the song “Pop Another Pill,” which received massive recognition and over 1 million views on YouTube. The song increased his popularity, and he got his breakthrough in the music industry.

After his success with Lil Wyte, Jelly Roll continued working on his music and released several mixtapes, including the Gamblin on a Whiteboy and Therapeutic Music series. 

In 2011, he collaborated with DJ Paul and Juicy J on the SNO group album, “Year-Round,” where he featured on the song “Come Here White Girl,” which was named as one of the top memorable collaborations between white rappers by XXL magazine. 

Jelly Roll then released his debut solo studio album The Big Sal Story, which he self-released on October 26, 2012.

Jelly Roll’s Musical Works

Jelly Roll has worked with notable names in the music industry, including Lil Wyte, Haystak, Struggle Jennings, Tech N9ne, Ryan Upchurch, DJ Paul, and Juicy J. 

He has released several successful mixtapes, which have received massive recognition in the music industry. Some of his popular mixtapes include the Therapeutic Music series, Whiskey, Weed, & Women, and The Big Sal Story. 

In addition, Jelly Roll has released numerous albums, including No Filter, Year-round, and Goodnight Nashville.

Jelly Roll’s Net Worth

Jelly Roll is one of the highest-paid rappers from Tennessee, with an estimated net worth of $6 million. He has amassed his net worth through his successful music career, which has seen him release several mixtapes that have been well-received in the music industry.

Jelly Roll’s Wealth and Luxury

Jelly Roll’s wealth has enabled him to live a luxurious lifestyle with his family. He and his wife, Bunnie Xo, plus their daughter Bailee live in a lavish house.

Jelly Roll’s Controversies

Jelly Roll has made headlines in the music industry, primarily due to his music and feuds with notable organizations like the Waffle House. 

The restaurant threatened legal action against him for using their name and logo on the cover of his song, “Whiskey, Weed, and Waffle House,” thus forcing him to rename the song “Whiskey, Weed, and Women.” 

Despite the controversies, Jelly Roll’s music career has remained successful and has continued to influence numerous upcoming rappers in the Southern rap music genre.
