Ree Drummond Children: Meet Ree Drummonds Kids Alex Drummond, Paige Drummond, Bryce Drummond, And

 Posted on July 21, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  314 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

American blogger, food writer, and television host of The Pioneer Woman, Ree Drummond, and her rancher husband have four children together — Alex, Paige, Bryce, and Todd.

Alex Drummond
Born on June 25, 1997, Alex attended Texas A&M University. In addition to helping her mom with filming her Food Network show, she works on photo shoots for The Pioneer Woman products and apparel, helps toward marketing efforts, and generally pitches in whenever the need arises.

Alex is married to Mauricio Scott, who works in a technology consulting firm. They both tied the knot on the Drummond family ranch in Oklahoma back in May, after announcing their engagement last August. The couple met during their freshman year of undergrad at Texas A&M University.

Paige Drummond
Born on October 31, 1999, Paige is the younger daughter of Tee and Ladd Drummond. She is currently in her final year at the University of Arkansas, where she is studying hospitality management.

At the age of 11, Paige was thrust into the spotlight with her siblings as her mother filmed episodes of The Pioneer Woman for The Food Network. She was seen assisting her father on the family’s cattle ranch in Pawhuska, Oklahoma, in episodes that featured the children as they homeschooled and celebrated special occasions such as birthdays.

Bryce Drummond
Born in September 17, 2002, Bryce started college last January at the University of North Texas.

He currently plays football with the team at the University of North Texas.

He graduated from high school a semester early so he could start college a semester early and be able to make spring practice with the football team.

Todd Drummond
Born in 2004, Todd is the last amongst the Drummond children.

Todd is following his big brother’s footsteps and continues to play football at Pawhuska High School, with dreams of going to college and eventually playing “at the highest level.
